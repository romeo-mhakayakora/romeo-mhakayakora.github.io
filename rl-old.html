"Old" Reinforcement Learning (Classical Foundations)

A comprehensive 12-week study plan detailing the exact mathematical and algorithmic mechanics of classical reinforcement learning, from bandits and MDPs to fitted models and partial observability.

Week 01

Introduction

Core concepts of agent-environment interaction loop, rewards hypothesis, states, actions, history, and sequential decision making objectives.

Week 02

Bandit Algorithms — UCB, PAC

Exploration vs. exploitation tradeoffs in multi-armed bandits, Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) algorithms, and Probably Approximately Correct (PAC) sample complexity bounds.

Week 03

Bandit Algorithms — Median Elimination, Policy Gradient

Median Elimination algorithm for finding the best arm in PAC bounds, and basic Policy Gradient methods applied to multi-armed bandit settings.

Week 04

Full RL & MDPs

Transitioning from stateless bandits to multi-state reinforcement learning, Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) formal framework, Markov properties, and return/discounting formulations.

Week 05

Bellman Optimality

State-value functions, action-value functions, Bellman expectation equations, and Bellman optimality equations.

Week 06

Dynamic Programming & TD Methods

Policy iteration, value iteration, Temporal Difference (TD) learning, TD(0) prediction, and bootstrapping mechanics.

Week 07

Eligibility Traces

n-step Temporal Difference learning, TD(λ) algorithm, accumulating and replacing eligibility traces, and forward vs. backward views.

Week 08

Function Approximation

Generalization in large/continuous state spaces, value function approximation, linear models, and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) updates.

Week 09

Least Squares Methods

Least Squares Temporal Difference (LSTD) learning, Least Squares Policy Iteration (LSPI), and linear representation methods.

Week 10

Fitted Q, DQN & Policy Gradient for Full RL

Fitted Q-Iteration, Deep Q-Networks (DQN) architecture, experience replay buffers, target network freezing, and policy gradients in full RL settings (REINFORCE).

Week 11

Hierarchical RL

Temporal abstractions, the Options framework, multi-level planning, and hierarchical policy architectures for scaling RL.

Week 12

POMDPs

Partially Observable Markov Decision Processes, belief states, belief MDPs, policy solutions under partial observability, and history-based state representations.
