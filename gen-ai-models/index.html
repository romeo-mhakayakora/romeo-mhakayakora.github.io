Reference Guide

Generative AI Models

A from-scratch guide to the models behind modern generative AI: language models, diffusion, flow matching, vision-language models, RLHF, alignment, and the frontier of multimodal generation. Each chapter builds on the last—from foundations to the systems running today.

I write these chapters as I learn. Content will be added over time.

Table of contents

  1. Chapter 1 Foundations: Probability & Information Theory Entropy, KL divergence, likelihood, Bayes, and the statistical principles that underpin every generative model.
  2. Chapter 2 Neural Architectures: From MLPs to Transformers How information flows through a network: MLPs, CNNs, RNNs, attention, and the transformer that changed everything.
  3. Chapter 3 Autoencoders Learning compressed representations with encoder-decoder architectures.
  4. Chapter 4 Variational Autoencoders Latent variable models with variational inference and the reparameterization trick.
  5. Chapter 5 Autoregressive Models Next-token prediction, causal attention, and the GPT architecture family.
  6. Chapter 6 Diffusion Models: From VAEs to Score-Based Variational autoencoders, the reparameterization trick, denoising diffusion, score matching, and what makes DALL-E and Stable Diffusion work.
  7. Chapter 7 Flow Matching & Continuous Normalizing Flows The generative engine inside modern policies: ODEs and SDEs, probability paths, the flow matching loss, and why it beats diffusion.
  8. Chapter 8 Generative Adversarial Networks The generator-discriminator game, training stability, conditional GANs, and where GANs still win.
  9. Chapter 9 Vision-Language Models CLIP, SigLIP, vision encoders, multimodal fusion, and how models learn to connect pixels and text.
